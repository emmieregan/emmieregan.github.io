About Me

Hi! I'm Emmie, a jack-of-all-trades designer from Springfield, MA. Some of my passions include game design, user interaction, and production. Studying Media Arts and Sciences with a psychology minor at Wellesley College has made me well-equipped to create digital environments that inspire and are inspired by the audience.

How I got into Design

One of the most common questions I get when explaining my areas of study is: "How did you get into that?" and "I thought you wanted to be a lawyer??!" Its true! I applied to college and dedicated the first 19 years of my life to be the Puerto Rican Elle Woods, but through my experiences in the field, I decided law wasn't the path for me like I thought it was. Looking for a new path, I found myself returning to my old hobbies like gaming, drawing, and making music. These creative endeavors were among the most consistent things in my life. I enjoyed creation, but I still craved the puzzle-solving of my Nintendo games and unsolved cases. Cue in computer science! I had always thought that technology was something unachievable growing up in a low-income city. I thought of coding as something cold and strict, rather than an iterative, satisfying process. Gaming served as a bridge between my artistic passions and analytical propencity. This eventually led me to take Intro to Game Design and where my interdisciplinary love deepened.

Game Design

One of my biggest passions and area of focus is game design! I have always been a gamer, but rarely saw people of my identity while playing. Because of this, I decided to make my own games based on my expereince as a student, a woman, and a Boricua! As seen above I'm made two smaller-scale games titled "Panda Portions" and "The Wellesley Effect". These projects were created using Unity/C# with both open-source and original assets.

Gosle!

Gamified Organization for Student Life and Engagement

My most recent project, Gosle! is a Wellesley student oriented application that creates a fun, accessible way for student organizations to create civic engagement tasks. It also helps students short on time to interact with organizations on the go. The pet-care aspect creates further incentives and honors Wellesley's unofficial mascot: the goose! Learn more here!
